College Algebra and Trigonometry Tools

I was chosen to be the UX designer on ALEKS' first agile development team. I designed, tested, and prototyped (Axure, HTML/CSS/JavaScript) math input tools which included complex user interactions like dragging, rotating, and graphing functions. I was the lead UX designer for an entire suite of Geometry and PreCalculus input tools.
Our process was as follows:

  • Collaborate with Math Content Department to create specification for a new tool.
  • Prototype the tool using intuition, content requirements, competitor analysis, and current interaction trends.
  • Moderate in-house usability tests with actual students.
  • Iterate on initial design if necessary.
  • Convert interactive prototype to development specification.
  • Collaborate with development to test bugs and find deviations from the specification.

QuickTables Integration

This was my first project. I took two separate products (ALEKS and QuickTables) and integrated them together for a seamless experience.

My goal was primarily to update interactions within QuickTables to be more consistent with the ALEKS redesign, integrate QuickTables navigation and reports into the ALEKS navigation menu and reports dashboard, and resolve space and layout issues for iPad compatibility.

ALEKS Student Module Redesign

Before I arrived the team conducted extensive user research and was in the process of updating the ALEKS student module. I was involved in several small projects to tie up loose ends or find solutions for edge cases.

Internal CMS Redesign
